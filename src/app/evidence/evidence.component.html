Climate Change Evidence (How Do We Know Climate Change is Real?)

There is clear and unequivocal proof that the Earth is undergoing an unprecedented rate of warming, and the primary driver of this phenomenon is human activity.

The rate of change since the mid-20th century is unprecedented over millennia.

Throughout the Earth's history, its climate has undergone fluctuations. Over the past 800,000 years, there have been eight alternating cycles of ice ages and warmer periods, with the conclusion of the last ice age approximately 11,700 years ago signifying the onset of the modern climate era and the rise of human civilization. These climate shifts are primarily linked to minute alterations in Earth's orbit, which impact the amount of solar energy reaching our planet.

The Fact That the Current Warming Trend is Real

The fact that the current warming trend is clearly related to human activity since the mid-1800s and is advancing at a rate unheard of in recent millennia makes it stand out as distinct. The atmospheric gases that human activity has released into the atmosphere are unquestionably to blame for trapping more of the Sun's energy within the Earth system, increasing warming in the atmosphere, oceans, and on land. As a result, several parts of the Earth's system, including the atmosphere, oceans, cryosphere, and biosphere, have seen quick and extensive alterations.

The use of Earth-orbiting satellites and cutting-edge technologies has allowed researchers to get a thorough understanding, accumulating varied data from around the world regarding our planet and its temperature over many years. This is relevant to the scientific consensus on climate change. These extensive data sets have shown significant trends and signals of a changing climate.

Scientists first discovered that carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases trap heat in the middle of the 19th century. In order to better understand how these gases affect the atmospheric transmission of infrared radiation, NASA has developed a number of scientific instruments. There is no question that increasing greenhouse gas concentrations cause global warming based on the consequences of these gases' observed increases in concentration.

The Evidence for Rapid Climate Change

  • Global Temperature Is Rising

    • Since the late 19th century, the planet's average surface temperature has climbed by around 2 degrees Fahrenheit (1 degrees Celsius), mostly as a result of rising atmospheric carbon dioxide emissions and other human activities. The last 40 years have seen the most warming, with the last seven years being the warmest. The warmest year on record is a tie between 2016 and 2020.F

  • The Ocean Is Getting Warmer

    • Most of this extra heat has been absorbed by the ocean, which has warmed by 0.67 degrees Fahrenheit (0.33 degrees Celsius) since 1969 in the top 100 meters (328 feet) of water. 90% of the surplus energy on Earth is kept in the ocean.

  • The Ice Sheets Are Shrinking

    • The mass of the Antarctic and Greenland ice sheets has shrunk. According to data from NASA's Gravity Recovery and Climate Experiment, between 1993 and 2019, Greenland lost an average of 279 billion tons of ice per year, while Antarctica lost roughly 148 billion tons.

  • Glaciers Are Retreating

  • Snow Cover Is Decreasing

    • Satellite observations reveal that the amount of spring snow cover in the Northern Hemisphere has decreased over the past five decades and the snow is melting earlier. Image credit: NASA/JPL-Caltech

  • Sea Level Is Rising

    • Global sea level rose about 8 inches (20 centimeters) in the last century. The rate in the last two decades, however, is nearly double that of the last century and accelerating slightly every year.

  • Arctic Sea Ice Is Declining

    • Both the extent and thickness of Arctic Sea ice has declined rapidly over the last several decades.

  • Extreme Events Are Increasing in Frequency

    • Since 1950, the number of record high temperature occurrences in the US has been rising while the number of record low temperature occurrences has been falling. Additionally, there have been more frequent instances of heavy rain in the United States.

  • Ocean Acidification Is Increasing

    • The acidity of surface ocean waters has risen by around 30% since the start of the Industrial Revolution. The reason for this increase is that people are releasing more carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, which causes more of it to be absorbed by the ocean. In recent decades, the ocean has absorbed 20% to 30% of all anthropogenic carbon dioxide emissions (7.2 to 10.8 billion metric tons annually).

Takeaways

  • Although the temperature of the Earth has varied over time, the current warming is occurring at a rate that has not been seen in the previous 10,000 years.
  • The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) states that "Since systematic scientific assessments first began in the 1970s, the influence of human activity on the warming of the climate system has evolved from theory to established fact."
  • Evidence of a changing climate is found in both natural resources (such as ice cores, rocks, and tree rings) and contemporary technology (such as satellites and devices).
  • There is ample proof that the globe is warming, from the rise in global temperature to the melting of ice sheets.
