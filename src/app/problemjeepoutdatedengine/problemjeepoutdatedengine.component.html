  • Is Jeepney Phase-out a Just Transition to Sustainable Transport System?
    - Addressing climate change challenges necessitates collaboration from all sectors, including transportation. Because fossil-fueled cars contribute to greenhouse gas emissions and air pollution, efforts to make transportation "greener" are being made. However, the societal effects are frequently overlooked. In the Philippines, jeepney drivers staged strikes to protest the phase-out of jeepneys in the name of sustainability.
  • Modernizing the jeepney fleet in the Philippines: Balancing Safety, Environmental, and Social Concerns
    - Removal of old age jeepneys would offer various advantages, especially in terms of safety and environmental impact. One big advantage would be a change to safer and more comfortable modes of transportation, which would reduce the number of accidents and injuries on the roads.
    - Old jeepneys are typically poorly maintained, and their engines create significant amounts of pollution. Furthermore, they are not constructed to fulfill modern safety regulations, putting passengers and other road users at risk. By retiring aging jeepneys, hazardous emissions could be decreased, improving the country's air quality. While jeepneys serve an important role in the country's transportation, a study conducted by the Blacksmith Institute and Clean Air Asia discovered that diesel-powered jeepneys produce 15% of total particulate matter emissions in Metro Manila.
    - Adoption of electric jeepneys, in particular, would not only cut air pollution but also provide passengers with a safer and more comfortable method of transportation. This would be consistent with the global goal of reaching net-zero emissions by 2050, which is critical in addressing climate change.
